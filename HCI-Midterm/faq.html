Overview

1. What is eFPS?

eFPS stands for Electronic Filing and Payment System, and it refers to the system developed and maintained by the Bureau of Internal Revenue (BIR) for electronically filing tax returns, including attachments, if any, and paying taxes due thereon, specifically through the internet.

2. What is e‑Filing?

e‑Filing is the process of electronically filing returns including attachments, if any, specifically through the internet.

3. What is e‑Payment?

e‑Payment is the process of electronically paying a tax liability through the internet banking facilities of Authorized Agent Banks (AABs).

4. Why do we need to use the eFPS? What are its objectives?

With eFPS, taxpayers can avail of a paperless tax filing experience and pay their taxes online via internet banking. It allows filing and payment anytime, anywhere with internet access.

5. What are the expected benefits of the system?

  • Convenient – quick, simple, and secure
  • Interactive – immediate online feedback
  • Self‑validating – minimizes errors before submission
  • Fast acknowledgement vs manual filing
  • 24/7 availability including holidays
  • Encrypted, secure data transmission
  • Cost‑effective processing

6. Who is eligible to use the system?

Mandated taxpayers required to file and pay taxes to the Philippine government, including corporations, agencies, top individual taxpayers, and others as defined by BIR circulars.

7. What do I need to get started with eFPS?

  • Computer with internet connection and supported browser (Firefox ≥40, Chrome ≥45, IE ≥11; 800×600 resolution+)
  • Email account for notifications
  • Optional: Adobe Reader ≥8, WinZIP/WinRAR, MS Office

8. When was the first implementation of eFPS?

eFPS has been available online since June 1, 2001.

9. Is there any additional cost that the taxpayer will incur upon availment of this system?

No. Enrollment and usage of eFPS is free of charge. Bank fees or minimum balance requirements may apply per your AAB.

10. What is the scope or coverage of the eFPS?

e‑Filing and e‑Payment for forms 0605, 1600, 1600WP, 1601C, 1601E, 1601F, 1602, 1603, 1604CF, 1604E, 1700, 1701, 1701Q, 1702, 1702Q, 1704, 2000, 2200A/AN/M/P/T, 2550M/Q, 2551/M, 2552, 2553.

11. Is the transaction with eFPS secure?

Yes. eFPS uses SSL encryption, VeriSign, firewalls, intrusion detection, antivirus, and two-level user authentication for secure transactions.

Enrollment

1. Where can I get information regarding enrollment procedures for eFPS?

Enrollment procedures are available on the eFPS webpage under the Job Aids link.

2. What are the steps in enrolling to eFPS?

Access bir.gov.ph → click eFPS → Enroll to eFPS → complete form → submit → await email approval (3–10 working days).

3. What are the requirements in availing of the system?

BIR‑ITS registration, board resolution authorizing officers, letter of intent, eFPS enrollment, and eFPS‑AAB enrollment.

4. How many days to know if enrollment was approved or rejected?

Expect an email within 3–10 working days.

5. As a large taxpayer, do I need to enroll my branches separately?

No. Only non-large taxpayers enroll branches separately; large taxpayers file consolidated returns via head office.

6. Can we submit more than one email during enrollment?

No. Only one email per user; up to three user accounts per TIN.

7. How to check if enrolled persons are authorized?

BIR verifies the Certification of Authorized Users submitted by the taxpayer.

8. Can I enroll more than once?

Initial enrollment is one-time. After activation, add users via Enroll More Users (max 3 users per TIN).

9. Do I need to enroll with an eFPS‑AAB after enrolling with eFPS?

Yes. Enroll separately with an eFPS‑AAB for e‑Payment.

10. Possible reasons for enrollment rejection?

Taxpayer found bogus, mismatch of info vs BIR records, or user not authorized.

e‑Payment

1. Can I enroll and open an account in any of the eFPS‑AABs?

Yes, any AAB capable of Internet banking transfers.

2. Do I need an existing account with an eFPS‑AAB?

Yes. Check bank fees/minimum ADB requirements.

3. Until what time do eFPS‑AABs accept e‑payment?

Cut-off times vary by bank. Inquire with your AAB.

Other FAQs

1. Can I view the return and the payment made?

Yes, via Tax Return Inquiry (up to 2 months).

2. What are existing eFPS Error and Application Messages?

Includes duplicate payment, parameter errors, missing FRN, connection issues, session timeouts, server errors.

3. Minimum PC requirements and browsers?

1.3 GHz processor, 256 MB RAM (1 GB recommended), Windows 2000+/XP/Vista+, IE6+/Firefox2+, 800×600 resolution, 128 kbps+ Internet.

4. What are the eFPS Security Settings?

Disable pop-up blockers or add efps.bir.gov.ph to exceptions, enable SSL 2.0/3.0, accept certificates, allow session cookies.

5. What if eFPS does not respond after submitting the payment form?

Check pop-ups and settings, clear cache, retry, or contact BIR Helpdesk.

Technical Requirements

System Specifications

  • Computer with 1.3 GHz processor or higher
  • 256 MB RAM (1 GB recommended)
  • Windows 2000/XP/Vista or later
  • Monitor supporting 800×600 resolution
  • Internet connection (128 kbps or higher)

Security Setup

  • Disable pop-up blockers or add efps.bir.gov.ph to exceptions
  • Enable SSL 2.0 and SSL 3.0 in browser settings
  • Accept site security certificates when prompted
  • Allow session cookies for the site
